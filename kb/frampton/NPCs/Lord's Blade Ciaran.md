**Lord's Blade Ciaran** is a Character in **Dark Souls****.**Lord's Blade Ciaran, often referred to simply as Ciaran, is one of the Four Knights of Gwyn, Lord of Cinder, and a member of The Lord's Blades. She embodies the stealthy, assassin-like archetype among the Four Knights. You can first encounter Ciaran at the Coliseum in Oolacile Township after defeating Knight Artorias.

### Information

Lord's Blade Ciaran, most commonly known as simply Ciaran, is one of the Four Knights of Gwyn Lord of Cinder and member of The Lord's Blades. She represents the classic thief-like character of the Four Knights.  She uses her signature Hornet's Ring and the Lord's Blade Set as well as the Gold and Dark Silver Tracers, which she offers you in exchange for Artorias's soul.  In combat, Ciaran uses flurries of weak but fast attacks and dodges in conjunction with throwing knives and Lloyd Talismans to rapidly wear down her opponents, much in the style she used to hunt the foes of her lord.

The Gold Tracer and Silver Tracer are both exceptional weapons (albeit with exceptional dexterity requirements), one being arguably the best weapon for inflicting bleed and unprecedently being the only weapon to gain a superior moveset when wielded in the left hand, the other being the weapon with the highest critical modifier and the nigh-unique ability to inflict toxic damage, making trading the relatively less useful soul somewhat common among players.

Her armor, the Lord's Blade Set, is effectively the midpoint between light and medium armor types, offering superior poise and defenses compared to light armor sets at the cost of slightly higher weight, while still being lighter then any medium armor set and having greater curse and poison resist, being an asset to almost any playstyle, making Ciaran's murder exceedingly common.

### Encounters

She appears next to a tiny grave in the place where you fight Artorias, after you defeated him (may require to reload or warp to a new area before triggering her spawn). She asks for the Soul of Artorias if the player has it, to pay tribute to his memory. If you give it to her, she will gift to you the Dark Silver Tracer and the Gold Tracer, claiming that she won't need them anymore. If you refuse, she will **not** turn hostile, and will remain where she is, and will speak to you again if prompted, giving you another chance to give her Artorias's Soul, but **will disappear forever** after you reload/go to another area. If you attack her, she will fight you, and **she will use** Lloyd's Talismans.

If you choose to give the Soul of Artorias to Ciaran, she will disappear after you reload or warp to a new area, and she cannot be found again until the next New Game cycle. This will prevent you from obtaining The Lord's Blade Set. If you wish to acquire all of her items and keep Artorias's Soul, it's recommended to kill her immediately after speaking with her or after hearing her dialogue and refusing her request. Many players suggest killing her on sight, as it's easy to accidentally select the wrong option and give her Artorias's Soul unintentionally. There are no known benefits, aside from moral considerations, for giving her Artorias's Soul instead of killing her. In exchange for the soul, she gives you her blades, Silver Tracer and Gold Tracer, which can also be obtained by looting her corpse if you choose to kill her instead.

### Combat

NOTE: Ciaran secretly has a spare Gold and Dark Silver Tracer. Giving her Artorias's soul to take her weapons so the player can easily kill her for her armor does not work.

Ciaran can be one of the more challenging NPCs to fight due to her formidable weaponry, decent health, impressive dodging capabilities, and the unique ability to use Lloyd's Talismans like the player. Her high damage output and ability to rapidly inflict bleed make her especially dangerous, with the potential to kill the player in just three or four chained attacks. For players on their first or second playthrough, it may take several attempts to defeat her, so it's recommended to rest at the Oolacile Township bonfire for quicker retries.

As with many initially passive NPCs, one effective strategy is to use Power Within or the Red Tearstone Ring to deal massive damage with your initial aggroing hit, aiming to kill her with the second or third hit. However, patience is often required in this fight, as attempting to spam attacks can result in her outdamaging you if she isn't staggered or if your stamina runs out before she's defeated. If two hits aren’t enough to take her down, it’s prudent to back off, recover stamina, and interrupt her during her attacks or as she closes in on you. Keeping your distance when not attacking is advisable, as she cannot heal herself.

For spellcasters, high-damage spells like Dark Bead and Homing Crystal Soul Mass can trivialize the fight, as they are difficult for NPCs to dodge. Pyromancers can use Fire Tempest while she’s still passive or rely on Black Flame or Great Combustion. Strength builds are recommended to use weapons with high stagger potential, provided they have the strength and endurance to wield them effectively. Faith-oriented builds should start with fast, high-damage spells like Wrath of the Gods, followed by buffing their weapon with Darkmoon or Sunlight Blade. Ciaran often dodges slower-moving single projectiles like Lightning Spears, but these can still be useful in combination with high-poise armor with bleed resistance to hit her mid-attack.

Equip armor with high bleed resistance, and consider using the Bloodshield or Bloodbite Ring, as bleed is Ciaran’s primary method of inflicting damage. Ciaran is weakest at range, with her only options being non-damaging Lloyd’s Talismans, which should be rolled through, and throwing knives, which can be sidestepped. Backing up to recover stamina usually prevents her from attacking, but be aware that she may throw knives or Lloyd’s Talismans when you create distance.

Due to her fast and lengthy attack chains with sweeping slashes, dodging her attacks while staying close without taking damage is challenging. If you choose to fight Ciaran in close quarters, it’s recommended to use a shield to maintain your guard, ideally one that can resist bleed. A shield with high stability will allow you to withstand her attacks without dropping your guard, and pairing it with a spear or thrusting sword enables you to retaliate without lowering your defense. Using a heavy shield can cause her attacks to bounce off, giving you easy openings to strike with a reasonably fast weapon.

### Trivia

* Her ring, Hornet Ring, can be found behind the gravestone of Artorias. you pick it up from a corpse that looks female, so it is possible that that is her body. When you meet her, she really wants to pay her respect to Artorias, so it is possible she died praying for him. Alternatively if you killed her that would also explain the location of her corpse.
* Curiously, Ciaran, contrary to appearances, speaks of humans as if she were not one of them, speaking of humanity as a whole when you fight her, as well as her dialog if approached with the Artorias Soul in storage or burned for souls instead of being on hand.  If this were the case, that would make her a race not seen elsewhere in the series, or otherwise a very short variant of the race that Artorias and the Silver Knights are composed of.
  + However, despite her claims, given her height and build, which is only ever seen in human characters in the entire series and the fact that she is succeptible to Dark Hand's lifedrain, which ONLY works on human opponents, it is highly likely that she is indeed human, which has interesting implications regarding her viewpoint and beliefs.
* As Ciaran dies, the line without subtitles "My... dear.. Artorias..." can be heard, suggesting she might have had a romantic relationship of sorts with him.
  + In Hawkeye Gough's cut dialogue, he exposes Ciaran indeed had romantic feelings for the knight, but they were unrequited.
