### Drops

* 50 souls
* Gold Pine Resin (5% Chance)

### Attacks

* Mushroom Punch.
* Mushroom Faceplant.
* Escape!! Once attacked they will run, and potentially lure you, towards their 'parents'.

* Just whack away at the little ones while they're down, blocking successful swings if necessary. Alternately, just let them walk past you and backstab them.

**Large Mushroom People** is an Enemy in **Dark Souls** and **Dark Souls Remastered**. Enemies are hostile creatures that respawn when players rest at a Bonfire or upon death. Special enemies that do not respawn are classified as Bosses, Mini Bosses or Invaders.



### Large Information

These enemies come in two varieties: small, largely-defenseless shrooms and enormous brutes. The little ones can attack, but will almost always fall flat on their faces instead, leaving them completely vulnerable. The large mushroom men deal tremendous damage with their slow wind-up punches, and can guard crush the player quite easily

 In Dark Souls 3 there is a pile of Mushroom People corpses in Farron Keep.

### Drops

* Gold Pine Resin (80% Chance)
* Gold Pine Resin x2  (20% Chance)

### Attacks

Warning, extreme damage, do not underestimate. Consumes a lot of stamina when blocked.

* Heavy Punch.
* Heavy Punch, followed up by a slow Heavy left hand punch.

### Strategy

In The Great Hollow you may be able to use the mushroom pads above to set up plunging attacks on the big guys; elsewhere, just try to get behind them and attack once or twice before rolling backward. Otherwise, lure them into swinging at you, then rush in for no more than two quick hits (depending on your weapon) before dodging back again. Be wary of a one-two swing combination, which can easily kill you. Never attempt to fight the large ones in groups!
 When returning to The Great Hollow from Ash Lake, aggro the Mushroom People into following you back through the Ash Lake tunnel. The tunnel narrows a short ways into it and the Large Mushroom People can't advance any further down the tunnel. Attack them at that spot, and be sure to avoid the one-two swing.
 Also, if you follow the mushroom pads up quite a ways, I found the big guys were unable to advance past a certain pad with a branch crossing over it, like it was blocking the way.
