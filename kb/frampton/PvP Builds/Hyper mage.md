### Build Strategy

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Main elements in this build:   1. Pure damage from the Moonlight Greatsword. 2. Damage enhanced by the Red Tearstone ring when under 20% HP. 3. Grass crest shield used for increased Stamina regeneration. 4. 45 Dexterity as a main stat, increased spell casting-speed. 5. Crown of dusk is manipulated, increases 20-26% magic damage. A problem with the crown itself, is the defense, the defense is quite-low, but that's not the thing - it decreases magic defense by 30% making other mages a weakness. The magic damage increase also affects Pyromancies. 6. Moonlight Greatsword's magic defense increase by 40 Pt. which decreases the Crown's magic defense penalty. | When invading in the forest, you'll often find yourself in a "Ganking" situation, which means there's 2 or 3 people fighting-off 1 person, you can't get another invader as the max-players in one world is 4 players, hence you can't do anything other than NPC help or man-up and face them. But parrying generally aids you greatly; here's a tutorial for the "Setup parry."   * Block first attack * Parry via tapping LT/L2 * Assume you've successfully parried them, and tap R1/RB.  Requirements:   * A shield * Somewhat-good connection (It's not necessary to get new internet if yours is bad, but just hope for the best that the host has good connection) |

Moonlight Greatsword Moveset:

|  |  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| One-handed R1/RB | One-handed R2/RT | Two-handed R1/RB | Two-handed R2/RT |
| * Horizontal "regular" attack, easily parried. * Don't spam RB/R1 to avoid being parried, use it once; roll back. | * Vertical "special" attack, cannot be parried. * Blasts a green-projectile via slamming the ground. | * Vertical "regular" attack, medium-easily parried. * Avoid spamming; same tactic should be utilized similarly to the one-handed counterpart. | * Horizontal "special" attack, cannot be parried. * Similar to the one-handed version, just a larger attack radius and higher damage. |

* Upon encounter of a player casting Great magic barrier, cast CMW on either of these weapons:
* Great scythe+15 > Scales E with Strength and A for Dexterity; moveset similar to the Lifehunt Scythe.
* Balder side sword+15 > Scales E with Strength and A for Dexterity; moveset similar to the long sword.
* Iaito+15 > 300 Bleeding, scales E with Strength and A for Dexterity; moveset similar to the Uchigatana.
* Falchion+15 >Scales E with Strength, and A for Dexterity; moveset similar to the Scimitar.
* Flamberge+15 > 300 Bleeding, scales D with Strength, B for Dexterity; moveset similar to the MLGS.

- I'm aware that CMW deals magic damage, but will aid these weapons by adding over 300 magic damage.

- These are all mainly Dexterity-scaling weapons.
