**Avelyn** is a Weapon in **Dark Souls** and **Dark Souls Remastered**. **Avelyn**, a masterwork repeating crossbow that unleashes three full-damage bolts in rapid succession through an intricate mechanism. Crossbows are powerful ranged weapons that fire bolts, capable of high base damage and devastating headshots, though they lack the zoom function of bows. With no stat scaling, their effectiveness relies on elemental upgrades. They can also be fired instantly at close range for melee utility.

> Repeating crossbow cherished by the weapon craftsman Eidas. Its elaborate design makes it closer to a work of art than a weapon. Intricate mechanism makes heavy damage possible through triple-shot firing of bolts. but in fact each bolt inflicts less damage."

### How to Get / Where to Find

The Duke's Archives. Found in a chest on top of a bookshelf. There are three ways to reach it:

1. Stand in front of the stairs on the tiled floor above the bookcase, then walk up the stairs. Activate the stairs and wait for them to stop moving, then reload your game (do not get off the stairs). You will load back in at the spot where you were you stood before walking onto the stairs, but the stairs won't be there any more. Walk (don't run) directly forward and off the ledge. You will land on the bookshelf.
2. Rotate the stairs, then immediately run down, and off, when the lowest part of the stairs is above the bookshelf. This can be a hit and miss approach, so prepare to die. If you start moving down the stairs immediately, and hug the right side of the stairs the entire way down without stopping, you should be able to reach the chest fairly consistently. The timing seems to work out correctly as long as you have between 25-50% equip load.
3. **Online:** Wait on the tiled floor above the bookshelf while a co-op player rotates the stairs, then walk straight forward (don't run) to drop down onto the bookshelf.

### Notes & Tips

* Give to Frampt to receive 50 souls.
* Special: Avelyn fires 3 bolts in quick succession, all of which deal the weapon's full damage.
* After you open the chest, if you have not cleared out the enemies in the room prior to attempting to get the chest, it is strongly advised to warp back to a bonfire instead of attempting to clear the room. Immediately upon opening the chest, you will be pelted by arrows from the undead archers and spells from the channeler, on top of having already taken heavy damage from the fall. If you fall off the bookshelf to the right, it should give you enough time to warp back to a bonfire. The reason for this is if you happen to die after getting the chest, there is a possibility that your souls will spawn on top of the bookshelf, even if you died on ground level. And you really don't want to have to get back onto that bookshelf ***again***, especially if your bloodstain holds a lot of Humanity.
