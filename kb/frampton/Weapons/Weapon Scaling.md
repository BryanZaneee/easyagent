# Weapon Scaling

| Weapon Scaling |
| --- |
| weapon scaling dks |
| The bonus to your total Attack Rating given by your weapon via a special formula based on how much points you put into a stat |

**Weapon scaling** is a gameplay mechanic in **Dark Souls** and **Dark Souls Remastered**. Most weapons in the game present an Attack Rating, plus a "bonus" for specific Stats that the player can level up to improve said bonus.

### Understanding your Weapon

Weapons show players several categories of information: **Attack Values (**also considered as **base damage or AR)**, **Parameter Bonuses**, **Damage Reduction (%)** and **Stability.**

* There are 4 **Attack Values/Base Damage:**physical damage Physical Damage, magic damage Magic Damage, lightning damage Lightning Damage and fire damage Fire Damage.
* There are 5 **Scaling Values/Parameter Bonus:**strengthStrength, dexterityDexterity, intelligenceIntelligence, faithFaith and Humanity.
* There are 4 **Auxiliary  Effects**: divine Divine Damage, occult Occult Damage, res bleed Bleed and poison Poison.
  + Some unique items also inflict auxiliary effects

### Parameter Bonus/Scaling Values

**Parameter Bonus/scaling values** refers to the bonus obtained from putting level up points into  strengthStrength, dexterityDexterity, intelligenceIntelligence and faithFaith. They are displayed as **Letter Value** that represents a range of scaling. Each weapon scales with at least one and/or more stats which will usually only grant one stat that heavily focus on scaling among them.  The four stats can be classified into two. **Physical damage** stats are  strengthStrength, dexterityDexterity which grant **bonus physical damage.** **Magic Damage** stats are  intelligenceIntelligence and faithFaith which grant **bonus magic damage**.

Table below explains the **Letter Value** that represents a range of scaling:
Weapons that has a letter S rank on any of that stat  strengthStrength, dexterityDexterity, intelligenceIntelligence and faithFaith would mean it has a good scaling on that particular stat.

| Letter | Scaling Range |
| --- | --- |
| E | 1%– 24% |
| D | 25%– 49% |
| C | 50%– 74% |
| B | 75%– 99% |
| A | 100%–139% |
| S | 140%–200% |

For **Physical damage. (**strengthStrength, dexterityDexterity)
The table below explains the scaling of the physical damage of the weapon. For example: For lower stats (from 10 - 40) points/level increase most, but once it reaches the soft cap at 40, it will start to slow down. Anything higher than 40 stat points would not give as much rating as when it was at the lower points (e.g. below 40 points).

| Stat level | Rating | Rating/level |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 10 | 5% | 3.50% |
| 20 | 40% | 2.25% |
| 40 | 85% | 0.25% |
| 99 | 100% | Unclassified |

For **Magic Damage.** (intelligenceIntelligence and faithFaith)
The table below explains the scaling of the magical damage of the weapon. For example: For lower stats (from 10 - 50) points/level increase most but once it reaches the soft cap at 50, it will start to slow down. Anything higher than 50 stat points would not give as much rating as when it was at the lower points (e.g. below 50 points).

| Stat level | Rating | Rating/level |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 10 | 5% | 2.25% |
| 30 | 50% | 1.50% |
| 50 | 80% | 0.41% |
| 99 | 100% | Unclassified |
