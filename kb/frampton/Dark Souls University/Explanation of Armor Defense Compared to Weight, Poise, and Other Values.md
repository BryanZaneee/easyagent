# Explanation of Armor Defense Compared to Weight, Poise, and Other Values

Some armor gives very high defense but is very heavy by comparison, causing the character to move very slowly. Also, some armor gives very high poise, allowing you to resist being interrupted by attacks, but does not protect against incoming damage very well. This page shows the effectivness of a piece of **fully upgraded\*** armor (and entire armor sets) compared to its weight and the amount of poise it gives. As such, this list is intended to give an overall sense of how "good" the armor is when comparing its benefits (defense rating and poise) to its drawbacks (equip burden based on weight). This comparison only covers physical defense, and does *not* take into account elemental defense (magic, fire, and lightning) or conditional defense (poison, bleed, and curse). These attributes will be covered later.

 ( **\*** The majority of the defense values listed below are not actually upgraded numbers.)

 Please see the armor page for a complete listing of armor sets and their corresponding values.

 This list is incomplete, and does not contain all armor sets.

 Each entry is comprised of a defense per pounds rating ( **def/lbs**), which is the defense of a piece versus how heavy it is. In this value, **a higher rating is better.**
 Entries also have a defense per poise rating ( **def/poise**), which is how well they defend versus how much poise they offer. In this value, **a lower rating is better**.

###### **Best Def/Poise Rating:** Crystal Armor, with a rating of 1.75 def/poise

This list is organized as similarly as possible to armor page for easy cross-reference.
