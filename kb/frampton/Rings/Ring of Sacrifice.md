**Ring of Sacrifice** is a Ring in Dark Souls and Dark Souls Remastered. **Ring of Sacrifice**, created in a sacrificial rite to Velka, the Goddess of Sin, this mystical ring allows its wearer to lose nothing upon death, though the ring itself shatters. No bloodstain is left behind, but any existing unretrieved bloodstain remains available after the ring's use. Players can equip up to 2 Rings, but equipping two of the same item is not possible.

> This mystical ring was created in a sacrificial rite of Velka, the Goddess of Sin. Its wearer will lose nothing upon death, but the ring itself breaks.

### Effects

* Upon death, souls and humanity are not lost ("RING REVIVAL"), but the ring itself *is* ("Ring of Sacrifice shattered").
* No bloodstain will be left behind. If there already *was* an unretrieved bloodstain, it will **still be available to be retrieved** after ring revival.

### Locations

* Found on the way from Firelink Shrine to Undead Burg on a corpse in plain view on the lower level of the aqueduct..
* Found on the ledge above the well, in the central courtyard of Painted World of Ariamis.
* Sold by Oswald of Carim in the belltower of Undead Parish for 5,000 souls. He has a limited supply of 10.
* Trade one Humanity with Snuggly to receive one Ring of Sacrifice.

### Notes and Tips

* Does not trigger if you died from becoming cursed. Use a Rare Ring of Sacrifice instead of one of these if that is a possibility.
* DOES trigger (and shatters) if equipped as a phantom, even if a type of phantom that is normally not penalized upon death - so take it off prior to jolly co-operation!
* This ring reappears in *Dark Souls 3*.

### Application

* These are best used *after* having already dropped, when the bloodstain to be retrieved is in a particularly dangerous area and the player cannot be sure of getting out safely after picking it up. By equipping a Ring of Sacrifice, the player can afford to make a suicide run at the bloodstain, guaranteeing a safe escape afterwards (possibly at the cost of the ring), or granting a second chance (definitely at the cost of the ring) if dropping before reaching the bloodstain.
* Just having one equipped "just in case" is subpar. Keep in mind that wearing this ring occupies a slot some other ring could be sitting in - one which gives a player a benefit that doesn't require dying to activate - which puts you at a disadvantage and makes it more likely the Ring of Sacrifice will be triggered at a time it really wasn't crucial. The rings are expensive and of limited quantity, so they should only be used when the soul/humanity loss would be devastating.

### Trivia

* An early iteration of the Ring of sacrifice is in the game Eternal ring where it functions similarly but instead of preventing item loss it just prevents death.
